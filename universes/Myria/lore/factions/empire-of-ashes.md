The oldest state on Myria — some twenty-five centuries by its own count. Founded by the Fifteen, a band of exceptional generals, scholars and rulers whose imperial cult is still the state religion; temples to each founder stand in every city, and folk swear some of the Fifteen were more than human.

The Empire despises magic — an old hatred born of its endless feud with the Aicamed Federation, hardened over centuries into deep popular superstition. It compensates with iron: the continent's most disciplined heavy infantry, military rail lines, primitive armoured engines and standardised artillery. Society is a strict pyramid — Emperor, nobility descended from the founders, the rising caste of engineers and officers, then everyone else.

The Empire was lately shaken to its roots: Arven Deymar, a commoner, took the Ash Throne in an audacious coup. And the forges are louder than ever — rumour says revolutionary great guns, built under exclusive Coalition contract, are months from delivery. Every chancellery on Myria is counting those months.
