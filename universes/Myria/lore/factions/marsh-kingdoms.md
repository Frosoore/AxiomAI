The deep south is wrong, and everyone knows it. The jungles grow too fast and sometimes fight back; beasts come out of the swamps changed; sickness blooms and vanishes by rules no physician can name. Most peoples fled those lands long ago. The Marsh Kingdoms stayed.

Their cities learned to tame what others fled: alchemy and living craft found nowhere else on Myria, medicines brewed from things best not described, and — the caravan tales insist — bred creatures that are half animal and half intention. Their harvesters bring up the south's strange organic crystals, which the Coalition buys by the crate through intermediaries neither side acknowledges.

Now and then something in the deep marsh grows powerful enough that the locals stop calling it an animal and start calling it a god. These beast-gods are worshipped or dreaded parish by parish; some demand tribute, some protect, some merely watch. The Kingdoms regard them the way port cities regard storms: a fact of the land.
