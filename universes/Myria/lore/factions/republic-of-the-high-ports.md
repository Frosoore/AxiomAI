Six hundred years ago, the rival port cities of the western coast realised they could rule the sea lanes together instead of bleeding each other over them. The Republic they founded is now the trading heart of Myria: first navy of the world — steamships, ironclads, dirigibles — and the busiest harbours anywhere.

It is governed by the Council of Captains, an oligarchy of shipping dynasties. In principle any fortune can buy a seat; in practice the same families have held them for generations. Justice is fast and biased; the merchant guilds run their own, better-trusted courts. Commercial freedom is sacred — everything else is negotiable.

Port cities glow with crystal lamps and run on steam trams, but the contrast between the Upper Terraces and the dockside slums is brutal. The Republic is the Scientific Coalition's best customer and quietly tolerates its illegal laboratories. Its land army is mediocre; its wealth, espionage and fleets are not. Capital and crown jewel: Highport.
