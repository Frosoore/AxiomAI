Folk call them the Flesh Scribes, the Black Alchemists, the Men of the Gulf. The Coalition is not a state: it is a clandestine brotherhood of the continent's best scientists, mages and engineers — and an invisible superpower. It holds a near-monopoly on advanced knowledge: vaccines, machines, restored magic, and the hybrid marvels nobody else can build — runic weapons, crystal engines, implants.

It sells discoveries under timed exclusivity contracts, funds the great universities, and buys the brightest graduates with limitless laboratories and the promise that "no science matters less than another." Its creed, repeated by defectors and never denied: knowledge stands above nations.

Every power depends on the Coalition and none trusts it. The stories — bodies bought from morgues, chimeras in hidden vaults, whole laboratories underground — are officially rumours. Its headquarters is said to be the Antrum, a buried city somewhere in the borderlands between the Republic and the Empire, which no official map admits exists.
