Two centuries of sealed borders turned the Aicamed Federation into something the world had never seen: a nation where every citizen is a mage. Facing invasion across their indefensible, crystal-rich plains, its rulers closed the frontier and decreed that all would learn magic — buying spells from every nation, writing the duty to teach into law, and raising the bar of required mastery year after year. Those who could not learn did not survive the state's attention. Nearly half the population paid that price.

When the borders reopened, the world found ordinary farmers worth military mages and rulers of terrifying power — the Federation is governed simply by its strongest mage. Invading it would mean a guerrilla war against an entire armed population; no one tries.

Aicamedi are insular, formal, and viewed with a mix of awe and fear. Their feud with the anti-magic Empire of Ashes is five hundred years old and shows no sign of softening.
