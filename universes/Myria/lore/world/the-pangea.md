Myria is a single vast continent ringed by oceans that no ship has ever crossed. The high seas are lethally unpredictable, and sailors' superstition holds that "the dead gods live out there" — so the world looks inward.

The Continental Heart is fertile plain country, cradle of the first reborn civilisations and the most densely peopled land. The West is all cliffs and booming port cities, where the sea is tame enough for coastal trade. The Centre holds wide grasslands where magic hangs unusually thick in the air. The East is arid steppe, the road of caravans. The South is jungle and marsh — lush, wrong, and dangerous, full of warped beasts. The North is frozen mountains and hard, isolated tribes.

Where intact bunkers were found, power followed; regions without them have stayed all but medieval. Rumour insists that deeper bunkers, never yet found, still sleep under the known ones.
