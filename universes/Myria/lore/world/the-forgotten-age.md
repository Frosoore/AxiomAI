Every civilisation on Myria stands on ruins it cannot explain. Scattered across the continent lie melted fortresses, glassed plains and the bones of cities far larger than anything built since — evidence of an annihilating catastrophe in the deep past that no chronicle records.

What followed is called the Forgotten Age: thousands of years of bare survival and slow rebuilding, without writing for long stretches, without magic, without memory. The peoples of today descend from those survivors, though none can say what they survived.

Scholars working in the bunker archives whisper of stranger things — wars older than history, names that appear in no living religion — but such papers are sealed, disputed, and read by a handful of specialists at most. The faiths of the present age are young, plentiful and contradictory; most worship powers no one has ever met. Each nation counts years from its own founding or revelation, and no two calendars agree.
