Capital of the Republic of the High-Ports and the largest harbour on Myria. Highport climbs from the water in layers: the Docks, all cranes, warehouses, taverns and noise; Foundry Row, where the shipwrights and manufactories smoke; Lowtown, crowded and cheap, policed by private militias when it is policed at all; the Scholars' Quarter, with its reading-houses, print shops and translators' garrets; and the Upper Terraces, where the shipping dynasties live behind wrought-iron and discreet guards, around the Council Hall of the Captains.

The city runs on steam trams and crystal lamps, drinks from new aqueducts, and gossips in a dozen languages — a stranger draws no attention here, which suits many of its residents. Council constables keep the centre orderly without being everywhere; the guilds and great families fill the gaps in their own ways.

In Highport the standing joke is that everything is for sale twice: once openly, once honestly. These days the gossip is all of the Empire's coming great guns, and what the Council means to do about them.
