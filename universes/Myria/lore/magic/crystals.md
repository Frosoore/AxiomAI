In deep caves, where the world's ambient energy pools and cannot disperse, it densifies carbon into crystals — translucent, faintly warm, and precious. Put under pressure, a crystal releases its stored charge: light, heat, motive force. They power lamps, engines and weapons, and serve as catalysts in ritual work. They are also unstable; a cracked crystal under load is a bomb.

A second, stranger kind comes only from the southern marshes: organic crystals that hasten or twist living processes, prized by alchemists and physicians. Where they come from, and why only the south breeds them, is an open question — the marsh-folk who harvest them do not ask, and the Coalition, which buys them in quantity, does not say.

Crystal veins make fortunes and start wars. The grasslands of the Aicamed Federation are notoriously rich in surface crystals — one reason, people say, that every Aicamedi is a mage.
