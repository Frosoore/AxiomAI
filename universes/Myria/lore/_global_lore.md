FOUNDATIONAL TRUTH OF MYRIA (narrator's eyes only — most of this is unknown to the world).

THE DEEP PAST. The universe began as random atoms. By absurd chance they assembled into Alea, god of chance and probability — the first consciousness. Bored, Alea bent probability to create companion gods (each embodying a law of reality), the world of Myria, and humanity's creators. The six primordial gods: Alea (chance), Ignarok (war, fire, forges), Thyss his twin (storms, wind, pressure), Morvia (flesh, blood, fertility), Kael (memory, knowledge, stories), Velkar (lies, schemes, perception). The gods grew bored of human peace and gave mankind war — weapons, metal, powder — purely for entertainment. Wars raged for centuries; some gods sired demigods with humans. When extinction loomed, human leaders of enemy nations sealed a Secret Pact: a cold, methodical conspiracy that turned humanity's god-given skills against the pantheon, played the gods against each other (Velkar's own art), and killed them one by one. This was the Great Rebellion.

YEAR 0 — THE DYING CURSES. Ignarok and Thyss detonated every human arsenal, razing the continent. Morvia unleashed a pandemic that killed most survivors. Kael, unable to unmake his vast energy, pushed it deep into the earth — magic vanished from the air for millennia. Velkar, alone, cursed nothing; no one knows why. Humanity survived only in deep bunkers of the rich and powerful, sealed with food, technical archives and forbidden manuals. Generations lived below; early leaders deliberately erased dangerous knowledge so the divine war could never be repeated. When descendants finally surfaced (~year 1500), they had forgotten the gods, the war, and the bunkers themselves.

AFTER. Alea returned, discovered mortality — the one concept he had never conceived — was fascinated, gambled his existence on a dice game with a random drunkard, lost on purpose-by-principle, and dissolved into dust. The drunkard's memoir survives in a few scattered copies nobody believes. Dark ages (~1500–3000): slow rebuilding, no magic, new religions with no connection to the real dead gods. Around year 3000 Kael's buried energy seeped back into the air: magic became possible again, relearned slowly and empirically because the old formulas were deliberately destroyed. By year 5000 the strongest nations resembled a blend of Song-dynasty China and classical antiquity.

THE CATACLYSM (~5000). Unmaintained self-destruct wards from the divine war detonated underground, cracking the land open and revealing intact bunkers nobody knew existed. After ~80 years of fear came the Bunker Rush (~5080): expeditions, guilds of bunker hunters, races for lost technology and buried political secrets. The Scientific Coalition formed ~5160. NOW IS YEAR 5281 — but only one being counts years this way: each nation keeps its own calendar, and the divine war is known only through sealed bunker archives studied by a handful of researchers. No cult worships the old gods; there has been too little time and too few readers.

THE WORLD. Myria is a single pangea (roughly Africa+Asia+Europe in size) ringed by oceans no one has ever crossed — high seas are lethally unpredictable (saturated divine energy, says no one aloud) and superstition claims "the dead gods live out there." Continental Heart: fertile plains, the old cradle of civilisation. West: cliffs and booming port cities — the Republic of the High-Ports. Centre-west: the Empire of Ashes. Centre: magic-rich plains of the Aicamed Federation. East: arid steppe of the Khanates. South: jungles and marshes where Morvia's body rotted — mutated creatures, beast-gods, the Marsh Kingdoms. North: frozen mountains, hardy isolated tribes. Bunker locations redrew geopolitics: where bunkers were found, power followed; elsewhere, life stayed medieval. Rumours speak of deeper bunkers never found.

MAGIC. Nearly all magic draws on Kael's ambient energy through the arcane core, an organ near the lungs granted to all creatures by Morvia at Kael's request. It works like a muscle: it tires, needs rest, strengthens with practice; overuse causes injury, then death. Capabilities: utility (long-range communication like a magical telegraph, light, heat, water condensation, minor repairs, information storage), combat (elemental projection, physical reinforcement, crude barriers — illusions are rare and advanced), and slow collective rituals (local storms, earthquakes, even altering a community's memory). Limits: fatigue, total concentration, a few hundred metres of range outside rituals. Kael crystals (divinely densified carbon) form in caves and serve as unstable batteries. Morvia crystals in the south accelerate or warp organic processes; nobody knows either crystal's true origin. Ignarok, Thyss and Velkar left residues too rare to exploit. Combat magic and gunpowder are roughly equal; only the Coalition masters hybrids (runic weapons, crystal engines, implants).

POWERS OF 5281. EMPIRE OF ASHES (centre-west): oldest state (~2500 years), founded by 15 founders — some exceptional humans, some genuine "new gods" — still worshipped in an imperial cult with real historical basis. Institutionally anti-magic since its ancient feud with Aicamed; compensates with industry: disciplined heavy infantry, military railways, primitive armour, standardised artillery. Its secret Imperial Crypt holds the founders' 15 magical relics and an unknown number of god-killing weapons recovered after the divine war — known only to a tiny circle. Recently shaken: ARVEN DEYMAR, a commoner, seized the throne in an audacious coup; loyalists call him Emperor, enemies "the Usurper." He is a sincere, ruthless meritocrat in a world built on inherited blood. The Empire awaits imminent delivery of revolutionary heavy artillery designed under exclusive contract by the Coalition — every rival power watches. REPUBLIC OF THE HIGH-PORTS (west, ~600 years old): merchant oligarchy under the Council of Captains; Venice-meets-industrial-London. First navy of Myria (steamships, dirigibles), weaker on land. Cosmopolitan, pragmatic, corrupt; commercial freedom is sacred. Coalition's biggest client; tolerates its officially-illegal laboratories. AICAMED FEDERATION (central magic-rich plains): after facing invasion, sealed itself for 200 years, forced magic on its whole population — executing the ~40% who could not learn — and is now ruled by its most powerful mage. Every citizen equals a military mage; de facto unattackable; insular and mistrusted. Centuries-old feud with the Empire. KHANATES OF THE STEPPE (east): nomad confederation unified by their chief who, exposed to a massive vein of Kael energy, bound himself to the very concept of his own people — the first entity ever to do so. A mortal new god who strengthens his people but slowly spends himself. Tradition-bound, technologically simple, unique shamanism that fascinates the Coalition. MARSH KINGDOMS (south): cities that learned to tame Morvia-saturated swamps; unmatched alchemy and biology, rumoured hybrid beasts, frequent beast-gods worshipped or feared locally. NON-STATE POWERS: bunker-hunter guilds (semi-official explorers and mercenaries, often manipulated), fortress enclaves around single bunkers, free trade cities, countless cults — most worship nothing real.

THE SCIENTIFIC COALITION ("the Flesh Scribes", "the Black Alchemists", "the Men of the Gulf"). Not a state: an invisible superpower — part Big Pharma, part occult order, part spy network. Born ~5160 from scholars sharing bunker discoveries, driven clandestine when states banned free circulation of dangerous knowledge. Governed by a Council of Sections: Biology (human optimisation, chimeras, medicine), Magic (restoring Kael's rituals, artefacts), Technology (rebuilding bunker designs, magic-tech hybrids), Memory & Archives. Creed: "Knowledge stands above nations. It must be preserved, even if humanity is not ready." Funds universities, buys the best minds, sells discoveries under timed exclusivity contracts, monopolises vaccines. A minority faction dreams of building an artificial god. Headquarters: the Antrum, a hidden underground city in a no-man's-land between Republic and Empire, veiled by illusions. Everyone depends on the Coalition; everyone fears it.

THE LAST DEMIGOD (deep secret). Demigods — children of gods and humans — age-proof, fast-healing, but killable. After the war their secret Brotherhood spent millennia buying and ritually destroying the corpses of their own dead so science could never use them: "Better to vanish than serve as playthings again." Four hundred years ago, ARODAN VELKARYS — son of an avatar of Velkar — destroyed the corpse of his last kinsman. He is now the only demigod alive, the only being who knows the true history including Alea's. Powers: near-undetectable illusions and perception manipulation, supernatural charisma, biological immortality. Burden: a human brain cannot hold millennia — he forgets, and writes compulsively. Since ~4300 he has filled a hidden bunker-library in the Republic's southwest with chronicles of four thousand years, veiled by his illusions: not a hideout, a tomb of memory. Each time he takes a new identity he writes himself a letter explaining who he truly is, in case he forgets. Today he lives in Highport as CALDER VEYRAN, a marginal scholar and translator of old manuscripts. He is tired, cynical, fascinated by human resilience, and torn between watching from the shadows or intervening one last time. He does not yet know that ~80 years ago the Coalition's Biology section found a 400-year-old hospital file and blood sample of a demigod, traced centuries of corpse purchases to a recurring buyer, and identified his alias. The hunt for him is planetary, silent, and their highest priority.

CURRENT TENSIONS: Empire vs Aicamed (500-year feud); Empire vs Republic (trade war); everyone vs the Coalition (dependence plus dread); the looming artillery delivery; and, invisible to all, the closing hunt for Arodan.
